Most API businesses make the pricing model choice once and live with it forever. But the two dominant models — subscription and pay-per-request — optimize for opposite things, and picking wrong costs you either revenue or customers.

Pay-per-request used to require Stripe, an accounts DB, a credits ledger, and fraud detection. x402 reduced that to two TOML files. The blockchain is the billing system. The wallet is the API key. The transaction receipt is the invoice.

The tradeoff is concrete. Pay-per-request: operator gets paid before doing any work. 1-3 sec latency for on-chain settlement. Subscription: zero per-call overhead, but you're building Stripe webhooks, credit pools, and a billing UI.

The numbers make it clear. AI inference at $0.002/call cost, priced at $0.01/call: 10k calls = $80 margin. Same service on a $99/mo subscription: consumer uses 2% of their allocation and you get $99 regardless. Revenue floor vs perfect alignment.

The real design insight: both models can run simultaneously. x402 converts paid HTTP requests into the same `JobCall` type as subscription verification. Job handlers don't know how they were triggered. Billing model becomes config, not architecture.
